The Earth, The Sub-systems, and the Rock-Forming Minerals

Terrestrial Planets

  • Made up of rocks
  • Slower Rotation
  • Closer to the sun
  • Smaller
  • Lacks rings and have less moons

Jovian Planets

  • Gaseous Planets
  • Larger
  • Rotate Faster
  • Have rings and more moons
  • Less dense

Earth: The Living Planet

  • Earth is considered as a home of simple single-celled organisms up to the most complex life forms including humans.
  • It is undeniable that the planet we live in is a rare planet as it is the only planet in our solar system that permits life.

Factors That Make A Planet Habitable

  1. Temperature: Life seems to be limited to a temperature range of -15°C to 115°C
  2. Atmosphere:
    • Earth and Venus are the right size to hold a sufficient atmosphere.
    • Earth's atmosphere is about 100 miles thick.
  3. Energy: With a steady input of either light or chemical energy, cells can run the chemical reactions necessary for life.
  4. Nutrients: Those with water cycle or volcanic activity can transport and replenish the chemicals required by living organisms.

Why is the Earth Habitable?

  1. It has the right distance from the sun. The earth is located in the habitable zone (aka Goldilocks zone) of the the sun. It is neither too cold or too hot for life.

  2. Protected from harmful solar radiation by its own magnetic field (aka geomagnetic field). Magnetic field that extends from the earths exterior out into space where it interacts with the solar wind.

  3. It is kept warm by an insulating atmosphere.

  4. It has the right amount of ingredients for life, including water and carbon.

Earth's Subsystems

Earth science looks at the entire planet as a system of interacting parts called subsystem.

Atmosphere

  • Composed of nitrogen (78%), oxygen (21%), and 1% of other gases like argon, carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, ozone, and other inert gases.
  • It supports life
  • Regulates the climate
  • Acts as both a blanket and a filter

Biosphere

  • "Bio" refers to living organisms
  • Sometimes called as the "Large Ecosystem"
  • The zone of earth where all forms of life exists.

Geosphere

  • The solid earth, consisting of the entire planet from the center to the core to the outer crust.

Hydrosphere

  • The water parts of the Earth
  • Includes oceans, glaciers, etc.

Rock-Forming Minerals

Minerals are the building blocks of rocks.
Minerologists use a set of criteria to determine wether a material is classifies as a mineral o not.

Characteristics of Minerals

1. Naturally Occuring: A Product of Earth's natural processes.
2. Inorganic: Must be a product of earths physical processes (like errosion and weathering).
3. Homogenous Solid: Has a definite shape and volume
4. Crystalline Structure: Atoms are arranged in order or increasing pattern.
5. Chemical Composition: Represented by chemical formula.

Hardness

Hardness is a measure of the resistance to localized plastic deformation, such as an indentation or a scratch, induced mechanically either by pressing or abrasion.
