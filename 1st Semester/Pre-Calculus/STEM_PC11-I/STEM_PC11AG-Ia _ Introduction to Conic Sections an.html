Introduction to Conic Sections and Circles


Figure 1

Conic Sections/Conics

  • Particular class of curves which sometimes appear in nature.
  • A circle is a conic.
  • When a ball is thrown in the air it's trajectory is a parabola (parabolic path).
  • When a planet orbits a star the path is an eclipse.

Overview of Conic Sections

*Refer to figure 1

  • Circle = When the plane is horizontal
  • Eclipse = When the (tilted) plane intersects only one cone to form a bounded curve
  • Parabola = When the plane intersects only one cone to form an unbounded curve
  • Hyperbola = When the plane (not necessarily vertical) intersects
    both cones to form two unbounded curves (each called a branch of the hyperbola)

This lesson will focus mostly on circles. Leaving parabolas, eclipse, and the rest in other lessons.

Definition and Equation of a Circle

  • Set of all points in a plane that are at a constant distance.
  • Fixed point is called the center of the circle.
  • Connecting any point in the circle to the middle is the radius.
  • Connecting any point from one to another within the circle is the diameter.
  • Radius is half a diameter.
  • Diameter is twice the radius
